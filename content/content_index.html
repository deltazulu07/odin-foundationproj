Cycling descents by difficulty

What goes up must come down.

 

Descending on a road bike can be fun or nerve-wrecking. Knowing the descents ahead of time makes the difference.

Cycling Descent Difficulty Rating
Easy. Gentle gradient, sweeping turns. Safest road and traffic conditions.
Intermediate. Moderate gradient, technical turns. Road or traffic conditions require extra attention.
Advanced. Steepest gradient, narrow and twisty. Adverse road or traffic conditions.
Descending is the truest test of a cyclist's skill. It's where the exhilaration and the fear collide.
-Greg LeMond
